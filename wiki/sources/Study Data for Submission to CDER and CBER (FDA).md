## Summary

Official FDA landing page for study data submission requirements. Data standards are mandatory for NDAs, ANDAs, BLAs, INDs, and subsequent submissions. Non-conforming data may be refused at filing. Covers SDTM, ADaM, SEND, and Define-XML as currently required standards. FDA can refuse to file if electronic study data does not meet Data Standards Catalog requirements.

## Key Points

- **Technical Rejection Criteria**: FDA may refuse to file/receive applications whose study data does not conform to required standards (specified in Data Standards Catalog + Study Data Technical Conformance Guide)
- **Currently required standards**: SEND (nonclinical), SDTM (clinical tabulation), ADaM (analysis), Define-XML (metadata)
- **Update policy**: at least 1 year notice before requiring new version of existing standard; at least 2 years for entirely new standards
- **CBER specifics**: OVRR has its own technical specifications for vaccine datasets; SDSP (Study Data Standardization Plan) recommended for CBER submissions
- **Simplified ts.xpt creation guide** available for sponsors; video tutorial in R exists

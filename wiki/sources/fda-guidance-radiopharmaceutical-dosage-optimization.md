# FDA Guidance — Oncology Radiopharmaceutical Dosage Optimization

**Raw file**: `raw/sources/Oncology Therapeutic Radiopharmaceuticals Dosage Optimization.md`  
**Author(s)**: FDA Office of the Commissioner  
**Year**: 2025  
**Type**: regulatory-guidance  
**Venue**: FDA Guidance Documents

## Summary

This FDA guidance assists sponsors in identifying an optimized dosage — both administered activity and dosing schedule — for radiopharmaceutical therapies (RPTs) in oncology. It builds on the 2024 guidance on optimizing oncology drug dosages generally but addresses unique RPT considerations. The guidance covers the clinical development phase prior to submitting a marketing application.

## Key Points

- Provides a framework for **dosage optimization** specific to RPTs: administered activity (in GBq/MBq) and schedule, not a fixed mg/kg dose like conventional drugs
- Complements the August 2024 FDA guidance on *Optimizing the Dosage of Human Prescription Drugs and Biological Products for the Treatment of Oncologic Diseases*
- Scope is limited: does **not** address initial first-in-human administered activity selection, dosimetry software selection, or theranostic co-development strategy
- Relevant for theranostic pairs (e.g., diagnostic companion paired with therapeutic RPT)

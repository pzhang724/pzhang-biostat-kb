# Sponsor Responsibilities — IND Safety Reporting

**Raw file**: `raw/sources/Sponsor Responsibilities - Safety Reporting Requirements and Safety As.md`  
**Author(s)**: FDA Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER)  
**Year**: 2024  
**Type**: regulatory-guidance  
**Venue**: FDA Guidance Documents

## Summary

This FDA guidance document provides recommendations for sponsors and sponsor-investigators on complying with IND (Investigational New Drug) safety reporting requirements under 21 CFR 312.32. It clarifies key terms, specifies when and how to submit safety reports, and provides guidance on two provisions that require sponsors to assess aggregate data across their IND studies. The guidance also covers safety reporting for bioavailability (BA) and bioequivalence (BE) studies.

## Key Points

- Defines when an adverse event qualifies as an **unexpected serious adverse reaction** requiring expedited reporting
- Clarifies the distinction between **7-day** (fatal/immediately life-threatening unexpected SAR) and **15-day** (all other unexpected SARs) IND safety reports
- Provides interpretations of terms: "serious," "unexpected," "associated with the use of the drug" (i.e., causality assessment)
- Addresses aggregate data assessment provisions: sponsors must periodically review all safety information across their IND program
- Covers BA/BE studies, which have specific reporting obligations distinct from efficacy IND studies

## Methods / Concepts Covered

## Notable Quotes or Figures

> "Sponsors and sponsor-investigators should submit an IND safety report for any suspected adverse reaction that is both serious and unexpected."

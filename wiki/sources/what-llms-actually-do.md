## Summary

Part 1 of "The Shiny Side of LLMs" blog series from Posit. Explains what LLMs are, how they function at a conceptual/technical level (without deep-learning theory), and what they are and aren't good at. Targets R/Python programmers who want to build LLM-powered applications but are intimidated by jargon (embeddings, RAG, vector databases, streaming tokens).

## Key Points

- LLMs are accessible to programmers without deep learning expertise; the key is understanding "what's happening under the hood well enough to start building"
- Series will cover: LLM concepts, interaction via R/Python APIs, embeddings/RAG/vector databases, building Shiny-based LLM applications
- Emphasizes: LLMs mimic human reasoning but have well-known failure modes; understanding limitations is as important as capabilities
